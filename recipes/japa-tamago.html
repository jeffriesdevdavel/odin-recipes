Japan Tamago

Japan Tamago is a traditional Japanese dish made from eggs. It is often served as a side dish or as part of a bento box. The eggs are typically beaten and cooked in a rectangular pan to create a layered omelette. The dish is known for its sweet and savory flavor, and it is often enjoyed with soy sauce or other seasonings. Japan Tamago is a popular breakfast item in Japan and can also be found in sushi restaurants as a topping for sushi rolls.

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, sugar, soy sauce, and mirin until well combined.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a rectangular pan over medium heat.
  3. Pour a thin layer of the egg mixture into the pan and cook until it is set but still slightly runny on top.
  4. Using a spatula, carefully roll the cooked egg layer to one side of the pan.
  5. Pour another thin layer of the egg mixture into the pan, making sure to lift the rolled egg to allow the new layer to cook underneath.
  6. Repeat the process of rolling and adding layers until all the egg mixture is used up.
  7. Once all the layers are cooked, remove the rolled omelette from the pan and let it cool slightly before slicing it into pieces.
  8. Serve the Japan Tamago with soy sauce or your preferred seasonings, and enjoy!
